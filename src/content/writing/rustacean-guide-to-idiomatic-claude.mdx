What this post will cover:

- Why Claude reaches for Python-shaped patterns in Rust by default — `Box<dyn Trait>` for everything, `.unwrap()` in production code, gratuitous `.clone()`.
- Prompts that flip the default: asking for ownership/borrowing decisions explicitly, requesting "no allocations on the hot path", and naming the lifetime arena up front.
- A tight feedback loop with `cargo check`, `clippy`, and `rustfmt` in the chat — when to paste compiler errors back vs. when to rewrite from scratch.
- The patterns Claude still gets wrong (lifetimes through trait objects, async cancellation safety, `MaybeUninit` initialization) — and how to catch them in review.
- A small library of go-to prompts for Rust review, refactor, and unsafe-block sanity checks.
