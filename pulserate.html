Optical Fingertip Pulse Rate Sensor

I was part of a group of 4 that built a circuit that measures pulse from a single digit (finger).

Due to blood pumping to and fro inside the finger, the digit's reflectivity periodically changes with time. the periodicity of heartbeat is captured by measuring the intensity of light (generated by an IR LED) reflected by the finger, detected by a PT333 phototransistor, continuously with time. The generated signal is then sent through a Op-Amp based ﬁltering scheme (band pass ﬁlter with a comparator) to minimize noise and obtain a binary wave. The pulse rate is simply the frequency of the above wave which can be found using a zero-cross detector or simply the average value in an 1 second interval.
