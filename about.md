<p style='text-align: justify;'>
My name is Sarthak Consul and I am a senior undergraduate at IIT Bombay majoring in the Electrical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. I am interested in leveraging machine learning to find solutions to prevailing issues plaguing mankind. I believe the current form of supervised learning, while achieveing remarkable performance, is not scalable to arbitrary applications due to its reliance of annotations. This has led to my goal of developing semi-supervised and unsupervised techniques of learning and applying them. This has led to my interests in Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Computer Vision, Applied probability and Signal Processing. I am interested in theoretical aspects of problems in these fields and would like to build tools that help solve real world problems.
</p>

I am currently working on using generative models for singing style transfer with [Prof. Subhasis Chaudhuri](https://www.ee.iitb.ac.in/~sc/){:target="\_blank"} as a part of my Bachelor's thesis. I am also working with [Prof. Amit Sethi](https://www.ee.iitb.ac.in/~asethi/){:target="\_blank"} to develop a Reinforcement Learning based approach for semantic and interactive segmentation.

In the summer of 2019, I had the opportunity to work with [Prof. Ralph Müller](https://biomech.ethz.ch/research/ralph-mueller.html){:target="\_blank"} at the [Institute of Biomechanics](https://biomech.ethz.ch/){:target="\_blank"} at ETH Zürich on leveraging deep-learning to segment lacunae from 3D micro-CT scans of trabecular human bone tissue. 

In 2018, I was a student researcher at Medical Deep Learning and AI Lab - IIT Bombay on using deep-learning for the segmentation of nuclei from various 2D-medical datasets.

I spend most of my time diving into books, videos and blogs across the internet looking for something new to learn and do. I am also fond of hiking, reading fiction and watching movies.
