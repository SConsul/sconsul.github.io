Screening of pre-cancerous lesions in Oral Cavity

Oral cancer is major public health problem in the Indian subcontinent, where it ranks among the top three types of cancer in the India. This is largely due to the widespread of chewing paan, tobacco, and areca, coupled with poor oral hygiene practices in rural India.
What makes Oral Cancer more deadly is the fact that often, there are mild symptoms, that often are disregarded, in the early stages. As such, in India patients only approach hospitals when the symptoms get severe.

Our goal is to make a screening model that can be run on mobile phones to remotely diagnose patients. This would allow timely intervention and treatment of patients, improving survival rates.

I have been part of team annotating and pre-processing data from arround 900 patients. I am currently working on segmenting the oral cavity from images which would be then fed to a neural network to classify the lesion.
